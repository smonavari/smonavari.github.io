I am a PhD Candidate in Quantitative Marketing at Warwick Business School, supervised by Dr. Iman Ahmadi, Prof. Nick Lee, and Prof.John Rudd.

My research examines how platform-mediated offers interact with consumer expectations, with a focus on promotional pricing strategies, digital platform design, and consumer health. Research

Working Papers

"Planned vs. Impulse: Discount Format"

With Iman Ahmadi, Nick Lee, and John Rudd  ·  Job Market Paper

This paper examines how planned (future-redeemable) versus immediate discount formats differentially shape consumer spending and purchase behaviour. Using a 17-month panel dataset of approximately 45,000 users from a digital platform, we show that the two formats operate through different primary intervention points, generating divergent effects on monthly spend and basket value.

"Decomposing Promotional Timing: When Discounts Are Given, When Rewards Arrive, and Why the Difference Matters"

Working Paper, 2026

A meta-analysis of effect sizes from academic papers (2017–2026) decomposing promotional timing into signalling (immediate vs. planned) and reward delivery (instant vs. delayed). Results indicate that reward timing is the primary driver of effectiveness, and that the disadvantage of delayed rewards is almost entirely offset when consumers receive advance notice, allowing for anticipatory mental accounting.

"Beyond Single LLMs: Using Model Ensembles to Improve Synthetic Data in Marketing Experiments"

With Mohammad Sheikhian, Marco Gregori, and Iman Ahmadi

We evaluate the extent to which multi-model synthetic data generation (ensembles) improves alignment with human behaviour across diverse marketing experiments, providing a more robust alternative to single-model simulations.
