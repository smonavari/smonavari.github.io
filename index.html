About

I am a PhD Candidate in Quantitative Marketing at Warwick Business School, supervised by Prof. Iman Ahmadi, Prof. Nick Lee, and Prof. John Rudd. My research is empirical and causal: I study how firm-led interventions shape consumer decision-making and welfare on digital platforms, and I care as much about identification as I do about theory. I work with proprietary platform data, meta-analytic synthesis, and large-scale survey experiments.

My primary stream uses quasi-experimental methods on large administrative panel data to examine how promotional design affects consumer spending and behaviour across the customer value distribution. A second stream develops LLM ensemble pipelines as recalibrated surrogates for human survey respondents, integrating synthetic and observed data through Prediction-Powered Inference to extend what limited primary data can tell us.

Research

Under Review

“Beyond Single LLMs: Using Model Ensembles to Improve Synthetic Data in Marketing Experiments”

With Mohammad Sheikhian Kazerooni, Marco Gregori, and Iman Ahmadi. International Journal of Research in Marketing, under review.

Using four LLM families (ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, Grok) across six published marketing experiments, we develop a framework that improves LLM-human correlations from r ≈ 0.08 to r ≈ 0.40 via multi-model ensembling, covariate recalibration, and response-style adjustment integrated through Recalibrated Prediction-Powered Inference (RePPI). The pipeline reduces standard errors by approximately 16% on average, corresponding to an effective labeled-sample-size increase of roughly 24%.

Working Papers

“Planned vs. Impulse: Discount Format”

With Iman Ahmadi, Nick Lee, and John Rudd. Job Market Paper.

Using a 17-month panel dataset of approximately 45,000 users and a doubly robust PSM-TWFE design, we find that immediate discounts generate three times the net revenue response per month across all customer value tiers, whereas planned discounts fail to increase net spend among low-value consumers due to budgetary absorption.

“Decomposing Promotional Timing: When Discounts Are Given, When Rewards Arrive, and Why the Difference Matters”

Working Paper, 2026.

A meta-analysis of effect sizes from academic papers (2017–2026) decomposing promotional timing into signalling (immediate vs. planned) and reward delivery (instant vs. delayed). Instant rewards significantly outperform delayed ones; however, this disadvantage is nearly eliminated when consumers receive advance notice, enabling anticipatory mental accounting.

Research in Progress

“Consumer Expectations and Monetisation on a Digital Health Platform”

With Iman Ahmadi, Nick Lee, and John Rudd.

Examines how monetisation strategy on a publicly oriented digital health platform shapes user engagement and trust, applying difference-in-differences and dynamic topic modelling to user session data and app-store reviews.

“Polling with AI: Can LLM Surrogates Supplement Probabilistic Sampling for Voting-Intention Research?”

With Mohammad Sheikhian Kazerooni, Marco Gregori, and Iman Ahmadi.

Evaluates whether LLM predictions can supplement a small human survey (N ≈ 1,200 Scottish residents via Prolific) to produce valid constituency-level estimates of voting intention for the 2026 Scottish Parliament election, combining human and synthetic responses through Prediction-Powered Inference.
